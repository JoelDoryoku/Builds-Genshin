Introduction

Razor is one of the most popular and powerful Electro DPS characters in Genshin Impact. His fast attacks, high burst damage potential, and easy-to-execute playstyle make him a great choice for players of all skill levels.

In this comprehensive guide, we will cover everything you need to know to build the best Razor in Genshin Impact. From artifacts and weapons to party compositions and combat rotations, this is the ultimate resource for maximizing Razor’s damage output.

Whether you just pulled Razor and want to get started building him, or you’re a veteran looking to refine your Razor build, you’ll find plenty of tips and strategies here. Let’s dive in and electrify your Genshin Impact experience with an unstoppable Razor build!

Razor’s Strengths and Playstyle

Before we get into specific builds, let’s first understand Razor’s key strengths and playstyle:

  • Physical Damage Carry: Razor excels at dealing fast physical damage via his normal and charged attacks. He relies on physical DMG for the bulk of his damage.

  • Self-Sufficient: Razor can generate his own Superconduct with his elemental skill to reduce physical RES on enemies. His burst also makes him immune to interruption.

  • Beginner Friendly: Simple but high-damaging attack combos and no reliance on reactions makes Razor a great choice for new players.

  • Frontline Fighter: Razor wants to stay on the field dealing damage with basic attacks. Swap supports in and out while Razor goes to town.

  • Lone Wolf Playstyle: Razor’s kit encourages you to keep him on the field to maximize damage. Quick swapping is not optimal for Razor.

With this context in mind, let’s look at how to maximize Razor’s potential through weapons, artifacts, party, and strategy.

Best Weapons for Razor

Choosing the right weapon can greatly boost Razor’s damage output. Here are the top weapons for Razor:

Wolf’s Gravestone (5-star)

The best-in-slot for Razor. It increases ATK and gives a huge ATK buff when damaging enemies with low HP. Its passive synergizes perfectly with Razor’s burst mode.

The Unforged (5-star)

Gives a large ATK buff and increased DMG against enemies with shields. A great option if you lack Wolf’s Gravestone.

Serpent Spine (4-star)

Excellent crit rate substat and damage buff that increases the longer Razor stays on field attacking. Must avoid taking damage to maintain max stacks.

Prototype Archaic (4-star)

A craftable option with ATK% substat. Its AoE burst effect works well with Razor’s fast attacking speed.

Blackcliff Slasher (4-star)

Good free-to-play choice with crit DMG substat. Requires executing low HP enemies to activate its ATK buff.

Snow-Tombed Starsilver (4-star)

Craftable claymore that boosts physical DMG. Good for pure physical builds but doesn’t buff Razor’s electro damage.

Aim for a 5-star weapon if possible. For 4-stars, Serpent Spine is best if you can avoid getting hit. Otherwise Prototype Archaic is a solid choice.

Best Artifacts for Razor

Razor’s best artifact sets maximize his physical damage output:

Pale Flame (4pc)

The best set for physical Razor. Increases physical DMG and ATK. At max stacks the 2pc bonus also applies an even larger physical DMG buff.

Bloodstained Chivalry (2pc) + Pale Flame (2pc)

Very close second option that’s easier to farm. You still get a physical DMG bonus and ATK% increase.

Gladiator’s Finale (2pc) + Bloodstained Chivalry (2pc)

Decent alternative to the above. Gives ATK% and physical DMG.

For main stats prioritize:

  • Sands: ATK%
  • Goblet: Physical DMG Bonus
  • Circlet: Crit Rate or DMG

Substats to look for:

  • Crit rate/DMG
  • ATK%
  • Energy recharge
  • Elemental Mastery

Build Razor with 25% crit rate minimum to consistently trigger Superconduct. Elemental Mastery is good for boosting Superconduct damage.

Best Team Compositions for Razor

Razor’s best teammates provide crucial support effects without stealing field time from our main DPS:

Core Team

  • Razor: Main DPS
  • Cryo Support: Kaeya, Rosaria, Diona. Procs Superconduct to reduce physical RES.
  • Sub DPS/Buffer: Fischl, Xingqiu, Beidou. Off-field damage and utility.
  • Healer: Bennett, Diona. Healing and buffs/shielding.

Ideal Team Composition 1

  • Razor - Main DPS
  • Kaeya - Cryo Support for Superconduct
  • Fischl - Off-field electro DMG and energy battery
  • Bennett - Healing, ATK buffs, and pyro resonance

Ideal Team Composition 2

  • Razor - Main DPS
  • Diona - Cryo Support, healing, and shields
  • Xingqiu - Hydro sub DPS, damage reduction
  • Zhongli - Shields, resistance shred, geo resonance

Flex spots can be filled by an Anemo character like Venti for crowd control and Viridescent Venerer shred, or Albedo for geo resonance and off-field damage.

Razor Gameplay Tips and Combat Guide

Here are some key tips to maximize Razor’s potential in combat:

Normal Attack Combos

  • Use full normal attack string for maximum damage. End with hold E for big damage.
  • Dash cancel earlier parts of combo to weave in more attacks faster.
  • Jump cancel the final hit to immediately begin a new attack chain.

Skill and Burst Usage

  • Use E press to apply electro before Q burst for Superconduct.
  • Cast Q immediately when energy is full to maximize uptime.
  • Weave in E press during Q duration for resist shred and electro energy.
  • Use E hold for I-frames or to end burst combo with big damage.

Combat Rotations

  1. Apply cryo aura for Superconduct
  2. E press > Q Burst > Normal attack combo
  3. Rotate supports: Apply buffs/debuffs, use skills/bursts
  4. Swap back to Razor and repeat

Advanced Combos

  • E press > Q > E hold > Normal combo > E press
  • Dash cancel between attacks to animation cancel
  • Jump cancel final attack in chain to reset combo faster

Razor Solo Carry Playstyle

  • Focus on keeping Razor on the field 90% of the time
  • Maximize Razor’s burst and skill uptime
  • Swap supports only briefly for cryo application, buffs/heals
  • Fun “berserker” playstyle that showcases Razor’s lone wolf thematic

Mastering these Razor gameplay fundamentals will let you unleash lethal physical DMG as a true electro wolf!

Razor Builds Summary

In summary, the key components of a top-tier Razor build are:

  • Weapons: Wolf’s Gravestone > Serpent Spine > Prototype Archaic
  • Artifacts: Pale Flame > Bloodstained/Pale Flame
  • Team: Cryo support, off-field sub DPS, healer/buffer
  • Playstyle: Maximize field time, smart skill/burst cycling

Invest in Razor’s normal/charged attack talents first and foremost. Level his skill/burst later to boost electro damage.

Aim for 25% crit rate minimum and a 1:2 crit ratio for consistent damage. ATK%, physical DMG, and crit stats are ideal for Razor artifacts.

Razor Builds Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about building Razor:

Should I go for 4pc Pale Flame or 2pc Bloodstained + 2pc Pale Flame?

4pc Pale Flame is Razor’s best set if you can proc and maintain the 4pc effect. But 2pc Bloodstained + 2pc Pale Flame is much easier to farm and performs very closely.

What talents should I focus on leveling up first?

Prioritize normal attacks > elemental burst > elemental skill. Leveling normal attacks gives the biggest damage boost for physical Razor.

Is an ATK or physical DMG goblet better for Razor?

Always use a physical DMG bonus goblet. Razor’s burst still benefits from the physical bonus. An electro goblet is only better in niche max-energy recharge builds.

Should I use 4pc Gladiator on Razor?

4pc Gladiator is not optimal anymore. You will get more damage from 2pc Bloodstained + 2pc Pale Flame due to the 25% physical DMG bonus.

How much crit rate does Razor need?

Aim for at least 25% crit rate to maintain Superconduct uptime. Then build additional crit DMG according to the standard 1:2 ratio.

Make him your primary DPS and watch those HP bars melt with proper Superconduct reactions and physical powerhouse attacks!
